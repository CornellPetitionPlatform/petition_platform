The rapid expansion of AI data centers in our county poses significant environmental and social challenges that must be addressed. These facilities consume vast amounts of energy, often relying on non-renewable sources, which contributes to climate change and strains our local power grid. Additionally, the construction of these centers frequently leads to the destruction of natural habitats, negatively impacting local flora and fauna.

 

Moreover, the influx of AI data centers can drive up local property values and living costs, making it difficult for residents to afford housing and participate in their community. The prioritization of these industrial developments threatens the character and integrity of our neighborhoods, pushing out long-time residents in favor of transient tech workers. It's crucial to protect our community from these disruptive changes.

 

<p By implementing restrictions on the construction of AI data centers, we can promote sustainable development that respects both our environment and community needs. Establishing thoughtful policies will ensure a balanced approach to growth, prioritizing the wellbeing of current residents while considering the environmental impact. Protecting our county is essential for a sustainable future for all.
