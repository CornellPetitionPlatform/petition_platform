The current state of employment hiring processes is fraught with inefficiencies, including ghost postings and job advertisements that serve no actual hiring intent. These practices waste candidates' valuable time and energy, as they invest their effort into applying for positions that may never exist or for which they are unlikely to be considered. This cycle of frustration not only discourages skilled individuals from applying but also undermines the integrity of the job market.

Implementing stricter laws around job postings would mandate transparency and accountability from employers. Every job advertisement should include clear intentions regarding the position's availability and the specific criteria for consideration, preventing companies from posting vacancies as a means to gather resumes for future use or simply to fulfill internal requirements. Such measures would ensure that applicants can trust the hiring process, leading to a more engaged and hopeful workforce.

Moreover, requiring employers to adhere to these regulations can streamline recruitment processes, benefiting both parties. Candidates would save time and resources, while employers would be able to sift through genuinely interested applicants who are suited for their needs. By promoting a fair job market, we can foster a work environment that respects the efforts of potential employees and uplifts the economy as a whole.
