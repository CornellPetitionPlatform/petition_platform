Access to safe drinking water is a fundamental right for every community. In our neighborhood, many water supply pipes are decades old and some are made with lead, posing serious health risks to residents. The presence of lead in drinking water can lead to developmental issues in children and various health problems in adults.

 

By replacing these outdated and hazardous pipes, we can ensure that every household has access to clean, safe drinking water. Investing in modern infrastructure not only protects the health of our community members but also enhances property values and promotes a better quality of life.

 

This urgent action is necessary to safeguard public health and foster a thriving, sustainable neighborhood. It is time to prioritize the well-being of our community by updating our water supply system for the future.
