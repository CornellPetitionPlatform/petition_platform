The safety of our children is paramount, especially when it comes to getting to and from school. Currently, many students must navigate busy streets with insufficient sidewalks, unclear crosswalks, and inadequate street lighting. These conditions pose significant risks, making it challenging for young pedestrians to travel safely to and from our middle school.

By adding more sidewalks, crosswalks, and streetlights near the school, we can create a more secure environment for our students. Proper infrastructure would not only encourage children to walk to school, promoting healthy habits, but also reduce traffic congestion around the area. Parents would feel more at ease knowing their kids can walk safely without the constant threat of accidents.

Investing in these improvements is essential for the well-being of our children and the community as a whole. Safer pathways to school will foster a sense of community, encourage outdoor activity, and uphold the safety standards our families deserve. It is time to advocate for these critical enhancements to ensure a safer future for our students.
