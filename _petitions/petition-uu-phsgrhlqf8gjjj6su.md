The mental health crisis in our community is escalating, and immediate action is needed. A fully funded 24/7 mobile crisis team, operating independently of the local police department, is essential to address these urgent needs. This team would provide specialized care and support to individuals experiencing mental health emergencies, ensuring they receive appropriate assistance without the added stress of law enforcement involvement.

 

Trained in de-escalation techniques, this mobile unit would be equipped to handle emergencies with compassion and understanding. They would also provide emergency transportation and facilitate psychiatric referrals, bridging the gap between crisis and care for those in need.

 

By establishing this team, the City Council can take a significant step toward safeguarding our most vulnerable community members. Prioritizing mental health care is a crucial investment in the well-being and safety of everyone in our city.
