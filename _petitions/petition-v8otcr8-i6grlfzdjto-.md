The F train is a vital artery for countless commuters, but its current speed often compromises safety and reliability. Reducing the train's pace could lead to more frequent stops and smoother journeys, allowing passengers to board and alight more safely, especially in busy stations.

 

A slower F train would also create opportunities for better service quality. Transit staff could better assist passengers, especially those with disabilities, while the gradual travel allows for easier communication among commuters, fostering a more cooperative atmosphere.

 

Public transport should prioritize safety and service over speed. By advocating for a slower F train, we can enhance the travel experience for all users, making the subway system more accessible and enjoyable for everyone.
