Daylight Savings Time (DST) is an outdated practice that disrupts the natural rhythms of our lives, causing unnecessary stress and confusion each year. Originally intended to conserve energy, DST no longer serves its purpose in our modern society, where technology has vastly changed how we access and utilize energy.

 

Research has shown that the clock changes can have negative impacts on physical health, mental well-being, and productivity. Many people experience sleep disturbances and increased rates of heart attacks and workplace accidents in the days following the time shift. The disruption is simply not worth the minimal benefits that might still exist.

 

Ending Daylight Savings Time permanently would provide a more stable schedule for families, businesses, and communities. It’s time to recognize that our lives are better without these biannual disruptions. Supporting the end of DST would lead to a healthier, more consistent lifestyle for everyone.
