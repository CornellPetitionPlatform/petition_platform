The recent trend towards deregulation of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) poses significant risks to public health and the environment. By reducing regulations, we risk compromising essential safety standards that protect our air, water, food, and overall health. A well-regulated EPA and FDA are vital in ensuring that our environment is safeguarded against pollutants and that the food and drugs we consume meet rigorous safety standards.

Moreover, the deregulation process undermines years of scientific research and public trust. When these agencies operate without stringent oversight, industries may prioritize profit over safety, leading to dangerous products entering the market and environmental degradation that affects countless communities. It's crucial to recognize that the well-being of citizens and the environment should take precedence over corporate interests.

Restoring effective regulations within the EPA and FDA will ensure that public health and environmental safety are prioritized. It is essential to advocate for a return to rigorous oversight that reflects our commitment to protecting ourselves, future generations, and the planet. Strong protections are not just good policy; they are a moral imperative.
