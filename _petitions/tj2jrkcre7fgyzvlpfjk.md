Providing nutritious meals to children in schools is essential for their growth and development. However, the current approach of offering "healthy food" options is not effective if students refuse to eat them. The majority of meals designed for health often end up in the trash, defeating the purpose of promoting good nutrition.

 

When children are not engaged with the food offered to them, we miss a critical opportunity to instill healthy eating habits. Instead of pushing unappealing options, schools should collaborate with students to create meals that are both nutritious and appealing. By introducing culturally relevant, flavorful, and varied options, we can foster a positive relationship with food.

 

It is time to rethink school meal programs to ensure that healthy food is not only available but also enjoyable. Every child deserves the chance to thrive through a balanced diet, and we can achieve this by making lunches that students look forward to, rather than ones they discard.
