PFOS (Perfluorooctanesulfonic acid) is a harmful chemical that has been linked to serious health issues, including cancer, reproductive problems, and liver damage. Despite the dangers it poses, PFOS has been found in our drinking water supplies, endangering the health of our communities. Urging action to remove PFOS from our water is not just a matter of public health; it is a fundamental right to safe drinking water.

 

Many communities are already experiencing the detrimental effects of PFOS contamination, and the problem is only getting worse. Federal and state agencies must take swift and effective measures to monitor and eliminate PFOS from our drinking water sources. It is crucial to invest in advanced filtration systems and technology that can effectively tackle this pervasive pollutant.

 

By prioritizing action against PFOS contamination, we can protect our families and future generations from the associated health risks. Ensuring access to clean and safe drinking water should be a top priority for all levels of government. Demand that our leaders implement immediate and comprehensive strategies to remove PFOS from our water supply and safeguard our communities now and for the future.
