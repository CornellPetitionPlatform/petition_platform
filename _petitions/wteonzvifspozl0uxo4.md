The current federal minimum wage of $7.25 has not kept pace with the increasing cost of living, resulting in millions of hardworking Americans struggling to make ends meet. Workers across various sectors are unable to afford basic necessities, including food, housing, and healthcare, as inflation continues to rise. Increasing the minimum wage is essential to ensure that all employees receive a fair wage that reflects their contribution to the economy and allows them to support themselves and their families.

An increase in the federal minimum wage would not only benefit workers but also stimulate the economy. When low-wage workers earn more, they have increased purchasing power, which can lead to higher consumer spending and, in turn, create more jobs. This ripple effect is vital for sustaining economic growth in communities throughout the country, especially in areas heavily impacted by poverty.

Moreover, raising the minimum wage is a matter of justice and equity. It addresses wage stagnation that has disproportionately affected marginalized communities and underscores the principle that work should provide a livable income. Ensuring a higher minimum wage is a crucial step towards fostering a fairer and more just economy for everyone.
