Access to affordable medical care for women aged 40-65 in Texas is not just a health issue; it is a matter of dignity and respect for all women during a critical time in their lives. Many women in this age group do not qualify for Medicaid or Medicare without minor children, leaving them vulnerable as they navigate the physical and emotional challenges of perimenopause and menopause.

 

These changes can have significant impacts on a woman’s well-being, yet too often, necessary treatments and resources remain out of reach. Addressing these gaps in care is essential to ensure that all women have the support they need during this transition.

 

Expanding affordable medical care will empower women to manage their health proactively, enhance their quality of life, and enable them to contribute fully to their families and communities. This is a crucial step toward health equity in Texas.
